Theory

1 What is DSA?
        Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA) are the core of computer science.
        Data Structures organize and store data efficiently (like arrays, stacks, queues, trees).
        Algorithms are step-by-step methods to solve problems (like searching, sorting, traversing).
        DSA helps us write optimized and efficient code — crucial for real-world applications and interviews.

2 How to Solve Problems in DSA
        The general problem-solving process in DSA involves:
        Understanding the problem (what is asked?)
        Breaking down into smaller steps (subproblems)
        Designing algorithm/pseudocode
        Drawing flowchart (visual logic)
        Coding the logic
        Testing with sample inputs
        Optimizing for time and space
